23. Hyphenated Word Navigation

When a hyphenated compound word wraps across two lines — the first half ending with a hyphen at a line break, the second half continuing at the left margin of the next line — the word selector treats the split as a single navigation stop. Both halves are always highlighted together. This chapter verifies that behaviour and the related edge cases.

Dictionary

english-no-syn

Note

Enable hyphenation in the reader settings before starting. Without it, compound words will not wrap at their hyphens and no target pair will appear.

Increase the font size to 16 pt or 18 pt before starting. A larger font reduces the number of characters per line, making it more likely that a compound word wraps at its hyphen. Navigate the page until you find at least one word that ends with a hyphen at a line boundary; its continuation begins the next line at the left margin. That split forms the target pair used throughout the tests below. If no pair is visible, try a larger font size or switch to portrait orientation.

The first half of the pair appears at the end of its line (high X position). The second half appears at the left margin of the line below (low X position). Because of this, pressing Down from the first half does not land on the second half — the navigator finds the word closest in horizontal position to the first half on the next row, which is a word somewhere in the middle or right of that row. To land on the second half via row navigation, press Down from a word near the left margin (low X) instead.

Test words

The paragraph below contains several long hyphenated compounds. At 16 pt or larger, at least one should wrap at its hyphen.

The well-established practice of cross-examination relies on a state-of-the-art understanding of self-determination and re-examination. Long-term planning and up-to-date information help avoid mother-in-law situations in an ever-changing, multi-disciplinary, decision-making environment. A well-considered, re-evaluation of the above-mentioned points is often all that is needed.

When seeking target pairs, also look for these three edge-case hyphen-position patterns. They may be more readily found inside dictionary definitions than in the paragraph above.

  • Hyphen at start of word only — the token begins with a hyphen and has no trailing hyphen (e.g. -ation, -ness).
  • Hyphen at end of word only — the token ends with a hyphen and has no leading hyphen (e.g. re-, anti-). This is the standard first-half pattern exercised by Tests A–E.
  • Hyphen at both start and end — the token carries a hyphen on each side (e.g. -o-, -re-).

Test A: Both halves highlighted regardless of which half the cursor is on

Both halves must be highlighted simultaneously whenever the cursor is on either half — whether it arrived on the first half via horizontal navigation or on the second half via row navigation.

Steps

  1. Open word-select mode on this page.
  2. Navigate using Right to the first half of the target pair (the half ending with a hyphen).
  3. Verify: both halves are highlighted — the first half on its line and the second half at the left margin of the next line must both be inverted.
  4. Now navigate to the second half via row navigation: press Down past the second half's line to the row below it, press Left repeatedly until the cursor is on the leftmost word of that row, then press Up. Because the second half is the leftmost word on its line, the closest-X algorithm lands on it. (Pressing Left directly on the second half's row does not work — it snaps to the first half instead.)
  5. Verify: both halves are still highlighted. The highlight must not disappear from the first half when the cursor is on the second half.

Test B: Horizontal navigation skips the second half in both directions

The second half must never be a stopping point for horizontal navigation. Going Right through the pair skips the second half; going Left back through the pair snaps directly to the first half, also bypassing the second half.

Steps

  1. Navigate to the word immediately before the first half on the same line.
  2. Press Right once. Verify the cursor lands on the first half (both halves highlighted).
  3. Press Right again. The cursor wraps to the next line. Verify it lands on the word immediately after the second half — not on the second half itself. The hyphenated pair must no longer be highlighted.
  4. Press Left once. Verify the cursor snaps to the first half (both halves highlighted again). It must not stop on the second half.
  5. Press Left again. Verify the cursor moves to the word before the first half. The pair must no longer be highlighted.

Test C: Row navigation can land on the second half

Pressing Up or Down may land the cursor on the second half. When it does, the cursor stays there — it is not forced to the first half. Both halves remain highlighted.

Note

To reach the second half via row navigation, approach from a position with low X (near the left margin). The second half is the first word on its line; it has the lowest X on that row. Navigating Up from a word near the left margin of the row below lands on it.

Steps

  1. Navigate to the row below the second half's line. (Press Down from anywhere near the pair, then Down again if needed, to reach the line below the second half.)
  2. On that row, press Left repeatedly until the cursor is on the leftmost word (the first word on the line).
  3. Press Up. The cursor moves to the second half's line. Because the second half is the leftmost word on its line, the navigator finds it as the closest match.
  4. Press Up again. Verify the cursor moves to the word above the second half — a word near the left margin of the first half's line — and not to the word above the first half (near the right end of that line where the first half ends). Row navigation from the second half must use the second half's horizontal position as the reference, not the first half's.

Test D: Row navigation after backward snap stays on the correct row

After pressing Left snaps the cursor from the second half to the first half (crossing a row boundary), pressing Up must not move the cursor one row too far. The navigator records the second half's row as the reference for the next row navigation, so Up references that row and moves one row above it — which is the first half's own row.

Steps

  1. Navigate to the word immediately after the second half. (Use Right from the first half — it skips the second half and lands on the word after it.)
  2. Press Left once. Confirm: both halves are highlighted.
  3. Press Up. Verify: the cursor stays on the first half's row (it may shift to a different word on that row near the left margin, but it must not move to the row above the first half). The pair must still be highlighted if the cursor remains on the first half.

Test E: Left from second half (arrived via row navigation) skips the first half

If the cursor arrived on the second half via row navigation, pressing Left once must skip past the first half and land on the word before the pair. The first half must not be a stopping point in this case.

Steps

  1. Reach the second half via row navigation as in Test C (navigate to the row below, press Left to the leftmost word, press Up).
  2. Confirm: the cursor is on the second half and both halves are highlighted.
  3. Press Left once. Verify the cursor lands on the word before the first half on the first half's row. It must skip past the first half in a single press — the first half must not be a stop.

Test F: Navigation rules hold for all three hyphen-position variants

Repeat Tests A–E for each of the three hyphen-position patterns described in the Test words section. Each variant must obey all the same rules as the standard case.

Steps

  1. Locate a target pair for each variant. The hyphen-at-end-only case is the standard case from the test paragraph above. Hyphen-at-start-only and hyphen-at-both-ends may require opening dictionary definitions or using a larger font size.
  2. For each variant found, execute Tests A–E in full on that pair and verify all expected outcomes hold.

Test G: Multi-select phrase includes both halves of a hyphenated pair

When multi-select mode is active and the selection range begins or ends on either half of a hyphenated pair, both halves must be highlighted as part of the phrase — even though the cursor always stops on the first half and the second half lies one index outside the anchor-to-cursor range.

Steps — range ending on first half

  1. Open word-select mode on this page.
  2. Navigate to a word two or three words before the first half of the target pair.
  3. Long-press Confirm to enter multi-select mode. The anchor is set on the current word.
  4. Press Right until the cursor lands on the first half of the target pair (both halves should already be highlighted due to the single-select rule).
  5. Verify: the first half and the second half are both inverted, along with all words between the anchor and the first half.
  6. Press Confirm to finalize the selection and look up the phrase. Verify: the looked-up term shown in the definition view (or "not found" popup) joins the two halves into one hyphen-free word — e.g. self-determination split across lines must look up as selfdetermination, not self- determination. The hyphen at the line break must not appear in the search term, and the term must not be duplicated.

Steps — range starting on second half

  1. Use row navigation to place the cursor on the second half (see Test C for the approach).
  2. Long-press Confirm to enter multi-select with the anchor on the second half.
  3. Press Right once or twice to extend the selection to words after the pair.
  4. Verify: the first half is highlighted in addition to the second half and the words to its right. The first half must not be left un-highlighted simply because it falls outside the anchor-to-cursor index range.
  5. Press Confirm to finalize the selection and look up the phrase. Verify: the looked-up term merges both halves into one hyphen-free word, as in the previous steps, and includes the words to the right of the pair (e.g. selfdetermination relies, not self- determination relies).

Steps — range spanning both halves

  1. Navigate to a word two or three words before the first half of the target pair.
  2. Long-press Confirm to enter multi-select mode.
  3. Press Right until the cursor lands two or three words after the second half — both halves of the pair are now inside the anchor-to-cursor range, not just adjacent to it.
  4. Press Confirm to finalize the selection. Verify: the merged hyphen-free word appears exactly once in the looked-up term — the first and second halves must not each contribute a separate, still-hyphenated token.

Test H: All navigation rules hold inside the dictionary definition view

The word-select engine operates identically inside the dictionary definition panel. This test repeats Tests A–G on a hyphenated pair found within a definition.

Steps

  1. From word-select mode on this page, navigate to self-determination (or any other hyphenated compound from the test paragraph) and press Confirm to open its dictionary definition.
  2. Word-select mode is now active inside the definition panel. Increase the font size if needed until at least one hyphenated compound in the definition text wraps at its hyphen. If the current word's definition yields no pair, close the panel and try a different compound word.
  3. Identify the target pair inside the definition text, then execute Tests A–G in full on that pair.
